Greetings, I'm Piklu Mallick, a physics enthusiast currently pursuing my Ph.D. in the Faculty of Physics at the University of Göttingen. Additionally, I'm proud to be a part of the vibrant research community at the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization (MPIDS), where I actively contribute to our collective understanding of these dynamic processes. My academic journey takes a unique twist as I delve into the captivating realm of mathematical modeling of infectious diseases. This field seamlessly bridges the worlds of science, mathematics, and public health, offering a captivating landscape for exploration.

My research is anchored in understanding the intricate connections between disease dynamics and societal variables. It's a puzzle that I'm deeply committed to solving. In this pursuit, I harness the precision of Bayesian inference as a powerful tool. Additionally, I navigate the complex terrain of dynamical analysis to unearth the hidden facets of these interconnected systems.
