About Me

I am a PhD researcher in the Faculty of Physics at the University of Göttingen and at the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization (MPIDS). My academic focus is on mathematical modeling of infectious diseases, particularly in the context of COVID-19. I am interested in understanding how human behavior and public health interventions influence the spread of infectious diseases, and how we can use mathematical models to inform public health policy and decision-making.

My work blends physics, data science, and public health, applying computational and analytical tools to reveal the intricate interplay between disease dynamics and societal responses.
